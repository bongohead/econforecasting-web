DESCRIPTION

The secured overnight financing rate (SOFR) is a major benchmark interest rate in the world economy. The rate measures the cost of overnight inter-bank borrowing rates for loans collaterized by Treasury securities. SOFR was created in 2019 as a replacement for the London interbank offered rate (Libor) following the 2012 rate manipulation scandal. In the U.S., all new loan contracts created in 2022 or later are required to use SOFR in lieu of Libor.

FORECAST MODELS

Our Market Consensus Forecast is generated by utilizing data on publicly-traded SOFR futures, where market participants can make bets on the future value of SOFR rates. Our model derives the mean market expectation from this data. The term structure is interpolated and smoothed using a Diebold-Li three-factor parametrization[1], generating the final forecast.

This forecast can be interpreted as the mean market-expected values of future SOFR rates.
